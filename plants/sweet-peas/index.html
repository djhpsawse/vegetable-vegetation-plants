How to Grow Sweet Peas

A guide for fragrant, colourful climbing flowers

About sweet peas

Sweet peas (Lathyrus odoratus) are beloved for their scent and delicate, colourful blooms. They’re climbing annuals that thrive with support and reward you with flowers for cutting all summer long.

Choosing the right site

  • Light: Full sun for best flowering.
  • Soil: Rich, moisture-retentive but well-drained.
  • Support: Provide canes, netting, or a trellis.
Tip: Sweet peas love deep soil — dig in compost before planting.

Sowing sweet pea seeds

When to sow

  • Autumn: For early, strong plants (in a cold frame or greenhouse).
  • Spring: Main sowing time — March to April.

How to sow

  1. Use deep pots or root trainers.
  2. Fill with quality compost.
  3. Sow seeds 2–3 cm deep.
  4. Keep moist and in good light.
  5. Pinch out tips when 10 cm tall to encourage bushiness.

Planting out

  • Harden off seedlings before planting.
  • Plant 20–30 cm apart.
  • Water well and tie stems to supports.

Looking after sweet peas

Watering

Keep soil consistently moist — they dislike drying out.

Feeding

Feed weekly with a high-potash fertiliser once flowering begins.

Training

Tie in stems regularly to keep plants climbing neatly.

Deadheading

Pick flowers often to encourage more blooms.

Common problems

  • Slugs/snails: Protect young plants.
  • Mildew: Caused by dryness — water consistently.
  • Aphids: Remove by hand or spray with water.

Harvesting sweet peas

Pick flowers every few days for the longest display and best fragrance.

Quick reference

  • Type: Annual climber.
  • Light: Full sun.
  • Sowing depth: 2–3 cm.
  • Spacing: 20–30 cm.
  • Flowering: Early summer to autumn.
