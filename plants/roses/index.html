How to Grow Roses

A practical guide for healthy, long-lasting blooms

About roses

Roses (Rosa species) are classic garden shrubs known for their beautiful flowers, fragrance, and long flowering season. With thousands of varieties, they can suit almost any garden — from compact patio roses to climbing varieties that cover walls and arches.

Choosing the right site

  • Light: Full sun is best — at least 6 hours daily.
  • Soil: Fertile, well-drained soil enriched with compost.
  • Airflow: Good air circulation helps prevent disease.
Tip: Avoid planting roses where old roses have grown recently — this prevents “rose replant sickness.”

Choosing varieties

  • Hybrid teas: Large, classic blooms on long stems.
  • Floribundas: Clusters of flowers, great for borders.
  • Climbing roses: Perfect for walls, arches, and pergolas.
  • Shrub roses: Hardy, bushy, often fragrant.
  • Miniature roses: Ideal for pots and small spaces.

Planting roses

  1. Plant in autumn or early spring.
  2. Dig a hole wide enough for the roots to spread.
  3. Mix compost or well-rotted manure into the soil.
  4. Place the rose so the graft union sits just above soil level.
  5. Backfill, firm gently, and water well.

Looking after roses

Watering

  • New plants: Water regularly during the first season.
  • Established plants: Deep watering once a week in dry weather.

Feeding

Roses are hungry plants. Feed with a balanced rose fertiliser in spring, then again after the first flush of flowers.

Pruning

  • Winter: Prune to shape and remove dead or weak stems.
  • Summer: Deadhead spent blooms to encourage more flowers.

Mulching

Apply a 5–8 cm layer of compost or bark mulch in spring to retain moisture and suppress weeds.

Common problems

Black spot

A common fungal disease causing black patches on leaves. Remove affected leaves and improve airflow.

Aphids

  • Spray with water to knock them off.
  • Encourage ladybirds and hoverflies.

Powdery mildew

White powdery coating on leaves — water at the base and avoid overhead watering.

Enjoying your roses

  • Cut flowers early in the morning for best vase life.
  • Remove faded blooms to keep plants flowering.
  • Many varieties produce hips in autumn — great for wildlife.

Quick reference

  • Type: Flowering shrub.
  • Light: Full sun.
  • Soil: Fertile, well-drained.
  • Spacing: 45–90 cm depending on variety.
  • Flowering: Late spring to autumn.
