How to Grow Sunflowers

A cheerful guide for tall, bright, wildlife-friendly blooms

About sunflowers

Sunflowers (Helianthus annuus) are fast-growing annuals known for their large, sunny flower heads and impressive height. They’re easy to grow, great for kids, and attract bees, birds, and other wildlife. With varieties ranging from dwarf types to giants over 3 metres tall, there’s a sunflower for every garden.

Choosing the right site

  • Light: Full sun — at least 6–8 hours daily.
  • Soil: Moist but well-drained, enriched with compost.
  • Shelter: Tall varieties benefit from a wind-protected spot.
Tip: Plant sunflowers along fences or walls to give them natural support.

Choosing varieties

  • Giant sunflowers: Can reach 2–4 metres tall.
  • Dwarf sunflowers: Compact, ideal for pots and borders.
  • Multi-headed varieties: Produce many smaller blooms.
  • Coloured sunflowers: Shades of red, bronze, and cream.

Sowing sunflower seeds

When to sow

  • Indoors: 3–4 weeks before the last frost.
  • Outdoors: Late spring, once frost has passed.

Sowing outdoors

  1. Prepare soil by loosening the top 20 cm.
  2. Sow seeds 2–3 cm deep and 30–45 cm apart.
  3. Water gently after sowing.
  4. Germination usually takes 7–10 days.

Sowing in pots

  1. Use small pots with drainage holes.
  2. Fill with multipurpose compost.
  3. Sow one seed per pot, 2 cm deep.
  4. Keep moist and place in a bright, warm spot.
  5. Plant out once seedlings are sturdy and frost-free weather has arrived.

Looking after sunflowers

Watering

  • Young plants: Keep soil evenly moist.
  • Established plants: Deep water once or twice weekly in dry spells.

Feeding

Add compost at planting time. For giant varieties, a light feed every 2–3 weeks helps support tall growth.

Support

  • Stake tall varieties with bamboo canes.
  • Tie stems loosely with soft garden twine.

Common problems

Slugs and snails

Young sunflower seedlings are a favourite snack. Use barriers, check regularly, and protect early growth.

Birds digging up seeds

Cover newly sown areas with mesh until seedlings appear.

Wilting

Often due to underwatering — sunflowers need consistent moisture, especially when young.

Harvesting and using sunflowers

  • Cut flowers: Harvest when petals just begin to open.
  • Seeds: Allow flower heads to dry on the plant, then collect seeds for roasting or bird food.
  • Wildlife: Leave some heads for birds in autumn.

Quick reference

  • Type: Annual flower.
  • Light: Full sun.
  • Soil: Moist, fertile, well-drained.
  • Sowing depth: 2–3 cm.
  • Spacing: 30–45 cm.
  • Height: 30 cm to 4 m depending on variety.
