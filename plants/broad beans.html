How to Grow Broad Beans

A simple guide for strong, early-season crops

About broad beans

Broad beans (Vicia faba) are hardy, reliable vegetables that grow well in cool weather. They’re one of the earliest crops you can sow and produce large pods filled with nutritious beans. They’re easy to grow and great for beginners.

Choosing the right site

  • Light: Full sun.
  • Soil: Fertile, moisture-retentive, well-drained.
  • Shelter: Protect from strong winds — tall plants can topple.
Tip: Dig in compost before planting to help retain moisture.

Choosing varieties

  • Aquadulce Claudia: Best for autumn and early winter sowing.
  • Sutton: Compact, ideal for small gardens.
  • Witkiem: Fast-growing spring variety.

Sowing broad bean seeds

When to sow

  • Autumn: Hardy varieties for overwintering.
  • Spring: Main sowing time — February to April.

How to sow

  1. Make a trench 5–7 cm deep.
  2. Sow seeds 20–25 cm apart.
  3. Space rows 45–60 cm apart.
  4. Cover with soil and water well.
  5. Protect early sowings from mice and birds.

Looking after broad beans

Watering

Water regularly during flowering and pod formation — this boosts yields.

Feeding

Broad beans rarely need feeding, but a light mulch of compost helps.

Support

  • Use canes and string to create a simple support frame.
  • Tall varieties especially benefit from staking.

Pinching out tips

Once the first pods form, pinch out the top 5–8 cm of growth. This helps prevent blackfly and encourages pod development.

Common problems

Blackfly

Pinch out growing tips and encourage ladybirds.

Chocolate spot

A fungal disease — improve airflow and avoid overhead watering.

Wind damage

Use supports to stop plants falling over.

Harvesting broad beans

  • Pick young pods for the sweetest beans.
  • For larger beans, wait until pods swell fully.
  • Older beans can be double-podded for tenderness.

Quick reference

  • Type: Hardy vegetable.
  • Sowing depth: 5–7 cm.
  • Spacing: 20–25 cm.
  • Support: Recommended for tall varieties.
  • Harvest: Late spring to early summer.
